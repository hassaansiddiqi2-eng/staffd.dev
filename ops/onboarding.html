STEP 01 · PRE-START
READ YOUR CONTRACT — ALL OF IT
TODAY
▼

You'll receive a contractor agreement from Staffd before your first day. Read every clause before signing. Not skimming — reading. Especially the IP, confidentiality, and non-solicitation sections.

Read the IP clauseEverything you build for a client belongs to that client. Not to you, not to Staffd. Even if you came up with the idea independently while working on their project.
Read the confidentiality clauseYou cannot discuss what you're building, what stack the client uses, or anything you see in their codebase — with anyone, including friends and family.
Read the non-solicitation clauseYou cannot approach clients directly for a full-time role or freelance work outside of Staffd for 12 months after the engagement ends. This protects everyone.
Sign and return within 24 hours of receiving itUnsigned contracts delay your start date. If you have a question about any clause, email ops@staffd.dev before signing.
Non-negotiable: Working without a signed contract is not possible. We will delay your start date if this isn't complete.

DAY -2
▼

Before your first day, every tool needs to be installed, tested, and working. Spending 30 minutes on Day 1 figuring out Zoom is 30 minutes your client sees you as unprepared.

STEP 03 · PRE-START
RESEARCH YOUR CLIENT BEFORE DAY ONE
DAY -1
▼

You will be working inside someone's startup. Know what they're building before you walk in. A founder who sees you've done homework trusts you in the first 10 minutes.

Read their website end-to-endWhat do they actually do? Who is their customer? What's the value proposition? You should be able to explain their product in one sentence.
Find their product on Product Hunt, App Store, or in the browserUse it if you can. Even 10 minutes using a product teaches you more than an hour reading about it.
Read the founder's LinkedInWhat did they do before this startup? What's their background? This tells you how technical they are and how to calibrate your communication.
Look up their tech stack if publicly availableStackShare.io, their job postings, or their GitHub if open source. Know what you're walking into.
Prepare two questions to ask on Day 1Smart questions show preparation. Bad questions: "What does your company do?" Good questions: "What's the biggest technical pain point you're hoping I can help with first?"

DAY 1 MORNING
▼

Your first Slack message to the client sets the tone for the entire engagement. Send this within 30 minutes of gaining access to their workspace. Use this template exactly — don't try to be clever.

This message communicates: I've prepared, I'm organised, I'm not going to be a burden, and I'm flexible. It makes the founder feel like they hired well.
Send the first message within 30 minutes of joining their Slack

STEP 05 · DAY ONE
MAKE YOUR FIRST COMMIT BY DAY 3
DAY 1–3
▼

You must commit code to the client's main branch by Day 3. Not a PR. Not a draft. Merged, committed code. This is part of Staffd's guarantee to clients. If you are blocked from doing this, tell us within 24 hours.

Why Day 3 matters: Staffd guarantees clients that their engineer will be committing code within 7 days of starting. Your Day 3 commit is what we need to verify this. It doesn't have to be a large feature — a fixed bug, a refactored component, a test added. Anything merged and real.
Get codebase access on Day 1 — follow up if not received by Day 1 EODIf you don't have access by 5pm on Day 1, send a Slack message asking for it. Then message COO at Staffd if still no response by the next morning.
Run the project locally by Day 2If you can't get it running, ask for help. Don't stay stuck for more than 2 hours without asking a question.
Make your first commit by Day 3Even if it's a small bug fix. The commit signals to the client: this person is real, they can work in our codebase, the placement is working.
Tell COO when your first commit is mergedSend the commit URL to ops@staffd.dev. This is important for our records and for the client's guarantee.
If you are blocked: Missing dependencies, broken dev environment, no access, unclear requirements — tell us within 24 hours. We will escalate with the client immediately. Do NOT stay blocked silently.

STEP 06 · ONGOING
THE DAILY ASYNC UPDATE — NON-NEGOTIABLE
EVERY DAY
▼

Every single working day, before you stop working, you send a daily update in the client's Slack. This is not optional. This is what makes remote work actually work. It takes 5 minutes. It prevents 90% of client anxiety.

DAILY UPDATE FORMAT — USE THIS EVERY DAY
"📅 EOD Update — [Date]

✅ Shipped today:
- [Specific thing 1, with PR/commit link if applicable]
- [Specific thing 2]

🔄 In progress:
- [What you're working on tomorrow, with expected completion]

🚧 Blockers / questions:
- [Specific blocker with what you've tried and what you need — or "none"]

Hours logged today: [X] hrs"
Clients don't need long paragraphs. They need to know: did something ship, what's next, is anything blocked. This format answers all three in under 2 minutes of reading.
✕
Bad daily update
"Worked on the dashboard today. Will continue tomorrow."
✓
Good daily update
"Shipped: user auth flow (PR #42 merged). In progress: payments dashboard — estimated tomorrow EOD. Blocker: need Stripe test keys, can you send? 6hrs logged."
Send your first EOD update on Day 1Even if you only spent the day getting set up. "Shipped: got codebase running locally, reviewed auth module. In progress: making first commit tomorrow. No blockers." is a valid Day 1 update.

STEP 07 · ONGOING
HOW TO HANDLE BEING STUCK
WHEN IT HAPPENS
▼

You will get stuck. That's not a problem. Staying stuck silently for more than 2 hours without communicating is a problem. Here is the exact protocol.

Try to solve it yourself for 1 hour maximumCheck docs, Stack Overflow, the codebase. Set a timer. After 1 hour, if no progress — move to the next step.
Send a specific blocker message in Slack — not a vague one
If no response in 2 hours, ping COO at StaffdWe can sometimes reach the client faster than you can, or know context about the codebase that helps unblock you.
BLOCKER MESSAGE FORMAT
"Blocked on [specific thing]. Here's what I know:
- What I'm trying to do: [clear description]
- What I've tried: [list 2–3 things]
- What I think I need: [specific ask]
- Impact: this is blocking [feature/PR], I can unblock myself if I get [X] by [time]"
Never say: "I'm stuck, not sure what to do." Always have a specific question. If you genuinely have no idea where to start, that means you need a 15-minute call — ask for one.

STEP 08 · ONGOING
STANDUPS AND MEETINGS — HOW TO SHOW UP
RECURRING
▼

Every standup, every meeting, is a chance to either build or erode trust. The difference between an engineer who gets a 6-month renewal and one who doesn't is almost always how they show up in meetings.

  • Be 2 minutes early. Not on time — early. This signals respect for the client's schedule.
  • Camera on. Always. Unless the client specifically says it's optional. Default to camera on.
  • Have your update ready before the call starts. What you shipped yesterday, what you're working on today, any blockers. Don't improvise in a standup.
  • Ask one smart question per meeting. Not more. One specific, prepared question shows you're thinking beyond your task list.
  • Never say "I'll look into it" without a timeframe. Say "I'll look into it and have an answer by 3pm EST today."
  • Follow up async on anything that was discussed. After every meeting, send a brief Slack summary of the decisions made and who owns what. Even if nobody asked for it.

STEP 09 · ONGOING
HOW TO HANDLE UNCLEAR REQUIREMENTS
WHEN IT HAPPENS
▼

Seed-stage founders give unclear requirements. This is not a flaw — it's the nature of early-stage companies. Your job is not to wait for clarity. Your job is to create it.

WHEN REQUIREMENTS ARE UNCLEAR — ASK THIS
"Before I start on [task], I want to make sure I build the right thing. A few quick questions:
1. What does success look like — how will you know this is done?
2. Are there any existing examples of what you have in mind?
3. Any edge cases or constraints I should know about?

Happy to share a quick wireframe/spec of what I'm planning before I start if that would help catch misalignment early."
This question pattern shows professionalism. It prevents you from building the wrong thing. And founders love engineers who think about the outcome before writing the first line.
Never build based on assumptions you haven't verified. An hour of clarification saves a week of rework. When in doubt, ask — then build.

STEP 10 · WEEKLY
THE WEEKLY STAFFD CHECK-IN
EVERY WEDNESDAY
▼

Every Wednesday, COO will send you a brief WhatsApp message. Reply within 4 hours. This is how we monitor the placement and catch problems before they escalate.

WHAT COO WILL ASK
"Weekly check-in — how are things with [Client]?
1. How's the relationship/communication? (1–5)
2. Any technical blockers?
3. Anything we should know about?"
  • Be honest. If something is difficult, tell us. We can help. If you wait until it's a crisis, our options are limited.
  • Rate relationship honestly. A 3/5 from you triggers a proactive check-in with the client from our side — we'll see if there's anything we can improve. This protects your placement.
  • Don't just say "everything's fine." If everything genuinely is fine — great. But if there's a friction point you're managing, tell us so we can support you.

STEP 12 · YOUR CAREER
HOW TO GET PROMOTED TO A BETTER CLIENT
ONGOING
▼

Staffd has clients at different stages — some are early seed, some are post-Series A with bigger budgets and more interesting technical problems. How you perform with your first client determines which clients you get access to next.

⬆️
What moves you up
Daily updates sent consistently. First commit by Day 3. Client satisfaction score 4–5/5. Proactive blocker communication. Renewals requested by client.
⬇️
What stalls your profile
Missed daily updates. Silent when blocked. Defensive when given feedback. Client requests replacement. Satisfaction score below 3/5.
  • Ask for a testimonial after 60 days. A written quote from your client goes on your Staffd profile and is shown to future clients. Good testimonials unlock higher-rate clients.
  • Tell COO what you want to work on next. If you want a more complex technical challenge, a specific stack, a Series A team — tell us. We match you to what's available.
  • Refer other engineers. If you know someone who should be on the bench, refer them. Referral bonus: PKR 5,000 if they pass all four stages.
  • Your rate is reviewed every 6 months. If your client satisfaction scores are strong, you can request a rate review. Come with evidence: commits, features shipped, satisfaction scores.
