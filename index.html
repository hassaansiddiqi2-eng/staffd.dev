The Filter

We reject 97% of applicants.
Here's exactly why.

"Pre-vetted" means nothing without proof. Here's the four-stage process every engineer on our bench has survived.

Stage 1
CV + GitHub Audit
Manual review of every application. CV, GitHub commit history, live projects, LinkedIn. If we can't see real code they've shipped in a production environment, they don't move forward. No exceptions.
✓ 30% pass rate
Stage 2
Communication Interview
45-minute live call focused entirely on English fluency and communication quality. Can they explain a technical decision clearly? Run a standup? Push back on a bad idea professionally? This kills more candidates than the coding test.
✓ 40% of Stage 1
Stage 3
Technical Assessment
Stack-specific written test. Not LeetCode puzzles — real-world scenarios your engineers will actually face. "Here's a broken React component. Fix it and explain your reasoning." Scored against a rubric by our senior engineers.
✓ 50% of Stage 2
Stage 4
Live Coding Interview
60 minutes, live, with a senior engineer. Build something from scratch under real conditions. We're watching how they think when stuck, not just whether they get to the right answer. Communication during the build is scored equally to the output.
✓ 60% of Stage 3

Real Questions

Things founders ask us
before they hire.

"I've been burned by offshore developers before."
So have we. That's literally why we built Staffd.

The problem isn't Pakistani talent — it's the process. Upwork dumps 500 profiles on you and makes you do all the work. We do the opposite. We vet before you see them, match on your stack specifically, and guarantee the fit. The risk is ours.
"What about time zones? Pakistan is far."
Our engineers work a flexible schedule that includes 4–6 hours of overlap with US East Coast business hours as standard. For async-first teams — which is most seed-stage startups — this is a non-issue. We confirm overlap requirements on the intake call.
"How do I know they can actually communicate?"
Stage 2 of our vetting is entirely dedicated to this. It's a 45-minute live call focused on English fluency, async written communication, and the ability to explain technical decisions clearly. More engineers fail on this than on the coding test.
"Are there long-term contracts or lock-ins?"
No minimum commitment. You hire on a rolling monthly basis. Scale up, scale down, or end the engagement — with 2 weeks notice. We're confident enough in our engineers that we don't need contracts to keep you.
